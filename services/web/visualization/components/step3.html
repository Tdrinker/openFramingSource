Label Frames with Content Analysis

In Step 3, you should draw a sample of documents and manually classify the documents into the frames identified from Step 2. The goal of this step is to generate ground truth labels, which you will need to build a machine learning model in Step 4.

We recommend that you use the method of quantitative content analysis (QCA) to label frames. QCA is a commonly used method in communication research. To perform QCA, a representative sample of text (or other type of content) is drawn, on which two or more trained coders apply a codebook protocol, which should include all of the variables (e.g., frames) for analysis and their definitions. Prior to coding the entire sample independently, coders are first trained on the codebook, and their agreement on how to apply the codes is measured with Intercoder Reliability (ICR). In QCA, high ICR – meaning that two or more coders consistently categorized the content similarly – also typically indicates high validity of the coded results. Once coders have reached an acceptable ICR (above 90% agreement or 0.70 Krippendorf α), they can code the rest of the sample independently.
